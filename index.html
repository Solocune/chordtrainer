How ChordTrainer Works

🤔 What is chording?

On a CharaChorder, you press several keys simultaneously and the device outputs a whole word at once. This trainer helps you learn those key combinations with spaced repetition.

🏋 Practice tab

A word appears — chord it on your device. The trainer ignores intermediate characters the device types before the chord fires and corrects itself via backspace. Only the final output is judged.

  • Adaptive — uses SM-2 spaced repetition. Words you struggle with appear more often; mastered words come back just often enough to confirm they're still known.
  • Wrong — focuses on your lowest-accuracy words.
  • Slow — focuses on words with the highest chord reaction time.
  • Random — equal probability for all words.

Session X/Y (Accuracy%) — X = words chorded correctly on the first attempt this session, Y = total attempts. Accuracy = X÷Y. When you backspace and retry, it counts as an attempt but not a correct first-try.

Streak — consecutive words chorded correctly without any correction.

AFK detection: If you're away from the keyboard for more than the AFK threshold (default 5 seconds, adjustable in Settings), or if you switch to another tab/window or click outside the typing field, that attempt is excluded from statistics entirely — so stepping away won't distort your accuracy or chord-time scores. The word still advances normally when you return and chord it.

When all words in Adaptive mode are scheduled for future review, you'll see a Congratulations screen. You can continue in Random mode or add more words.

⚡ Test tab

All N words are shown as a block — read ahead as you chord. Chord each word, then press Space to confirm it. If the word doesn't match, you stay on the same word: a chord hint appears and the input is cleared so you can retry. Use Backspace or Ctrl+Backspace to correct a partially typed word before pressing Space. The test timer starts on your first keystroke (not when the test loads), so taking a moment before you begin doesn't count against your WPM.

  • WPM (inter) — words per minute from when the previous word was confirmed to when this one was. Counts the natural gap between chords.
  • Chord Time — from your first keystroke on the word to correct confirmation (pauses >5 s are excluded).
  • Delay — time from word appearing to your first keystroke. Reflects chord recall time.
  • First try % — percentage of words confirmed correctly on the first Space press, with no backspace used.
  • ✓ First try — correct on first attempt. ⚠ Corrected — required a backspace or a second attempt after a wrong Space press.

If you walk away mid-test, any gap >5 s is not counted as typing time. A chart showing cumulative WPM over time is shown at the end. Press Enter on the results screen to instantly start a new test with the same settings.

📊 Statistics tab

Click any word row to expand sparkline charts showing its chord time and delay history. Use the sort options to find your slowest or most error-prone words. Click Reset on a word to wipe its SM-2 progress (useful if you've reprogrammed a chord).

Learned words — a word is counted as "learned" once its review interval reaches the configured threshold (default: 10 days). The threshold is adjustable in Settings → General. The chart shows cumulative learned-word count over time.

Practice time — total active practice time accumulated in the Practice tab. AFK periods (idle > AFK threshold) are excluded so stepping away doesn't inflate the counter.

Next Review — the SM-2 algorithm schedules each word for review after a growing interval (configurable early steps, then multiplicative growth). Overdue = the word is ready now.

⌨ Chord Hints

If you connect your CharaChorder (or import a JSON chord map), the trainer can show you which keys to press for each word. The hint appears above the word so you can see it at a glance. In Settings, choose when to show hints:

  • Never — hints are hidden.
  • On wrong chord — hint appears in practice mode as soon as you chord the wrong word; in test mode it appears when you press Space with an incorrect word (and you stay on that word until you get it right).
  • Always — hint shown immediately when each word appears.
  • After delay — hint appears automatically after N configurable seconds without typing. Useful when you're thinking. Resets whenever you press a key.

Important: any attempt where a hint was shown — whether triggered by a wrong chord or by the delay timer — is always counted as a correction (not a first-try correct). This keeps your accuracy honest.

On wrong chord and After delay can be combined — click both buttons in Settings to enable both triggers simultaneously. Never and Always are exclusive and clear other selections.

Connect via Serial in Chrome/Edge. Web Serial works on GitHub Pages (https://) and http://localhost — but not when opening the file directly (file://). Or import a JSON chord map: go to charachorder.io → Device → Backup → Library.

⚙ Spaced Repetition Tuning

In Settings you can tune how aggressively the SM-2 algorithm schedules reviews:

  • 1st / 2nd success interval — set the first two spacing steps after memory-only correct answers (defaults: 1 day, then 6 days). Lower these if the default ramp feels too steep.
  • Max interval (days) — caps how long a word can go before being reviewed again. Default 365. Lower it (e.g. 30) to keep all words fresher.
  • Initial growth factor — interval multiplier for new words (default 2.5). Lower it for more frequent repetition.
  • Set all words due now — immediately marks every word for review (useful after a break).
  • Bring forward by N days — shifts every word's next review N days earlier, shortening current intervals.

📝 Word Sets

Built-in English Top 200 and Top 1000 word sets are available — click + English Sets in the Word Sets tab. You can also generate a word set automatically from your loaded chord map (one word per chord). Only one set is active at a time.

💡 Tips

  • Start with a small word set (20–50 words) and expand as you master them.
  • Use Adaptive mode daily — it handles scheduling for you.
  • Use Slow mode when you want to deliberately drill hesitant chords.
  • Export a backup regularly via Settings → Export backup.
